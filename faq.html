FAQs

Got some questions?

We gathered the most common questions in our inbox to give you an idea on the things that you need to consider in setting up your own hydroponics system.

Hydroponics is the method of growing plants in nutrient solutions (with or without an inert medium such as soil) to provide mechanical support.
  • Wicking
  • Deep Water Culture (DWC)
  • Nutrient Film Technique (NFT)
  • Ebb and Flow
  • When plants are grown in a hydroponic system, they grow at a rate of 30% - 50% faster compared to traditional farming methods.
  • Because water in a hydroponic system is captured and reused, the systems use less water — as much as 10 times less water — than traditional field crop watering methods.
  • Hydroponics saves space. Crops can be planted very close to one another with a hydroponics system since root growth is limited.
  • A hydroponic system grows nutrient-dense produce in greater quantities. In fact, we've found that our hydroponic vertical farms can grow about 240 times more than traditional farming methods.
  • Aeroponics
  • Aquaponics
  • Monoculture
  • You will need a lot of space to start: Space is not an obstacle in your path of growing healthy food. You can even set up your hydroponics system vertically to save space.
  • It is expensive to start: You don't need to buy new materials to set up a hydroponics system. You can use old pvc pipes, wood, and food container to start. You can find in the #PAGE some ways to start without breaking your pocket.
  • The chemicals are harmful:The nutrients used in hydroponics are safe to use. Because we are not using soil to grow the plant, pests can't reach our plant. That means, your favorite fruit or vegetable are free from harmful chemicals that are usually found in pesticides.
  • Plants that grew in hydroponics are not healthy:Technically, hydroponic plants can be more nutritious. One of the main reasons for this is that you have complete control over your plants' nutrients. You can tailor the water and nutrient mixture to meet the specific needs of your plants, which can result in plants that are healthier and more nutrient-dense.
  • It is difficult to maintain:I won't try to deceive you by saying it is easy to set-up and maintain especially if you don't know where to start. Don't fret because we are here to help!
